Title: Gangs of Wasseypur
Rank: 179
Year: 2012
Rating: 8.2
Plot: Part 1:

The film begins in 1941 with Shahid Khan, a Pathan, who impersonates the feared dacoit Sultana Qureshi to rob British trains. This leads to his banishment from Wasseypur. He settles in Dhanbad and becomes a coal miner. Shahid's wife dies during childbirth, and in a fit of rage, he kills the coal mine's muscleman.

In 1947, India gains independence, and British coal mines are sold to Indian industrialists. Ramadhir Singh, one of these industrialists, becomes a powerful figure in Dhanbad. He hires Shahid as a muscleman but eventually betrays him, leading to Shahid's murder.

Shahid's son, Sardar Khan, grows up with a thirst for revenge. He learns the truth about his father's death and vows to avenge him. The film spans several decades, depicting the rise of Sardar Khan in the criminal underworld, his marriages, and the ongoing power struggle with Ramadhir Singh.

Sardar Khan's family becomes embroiled in a violent feud with the Singh family, with casualties on both sides. The film also explores themes of loyalty, betrayal, and the cyclical nature of violence.

Part 2:

The second part continues the story of the power struggle between Sardar Khan and Ramadhir Singh. Sardar's son, Danish, joins the family business, and the rivalry with the Singhs escalates.

Sardar Khan shifts his focus to illegal activities like sand and iron ore theft, becoming a feared and influential figure in Wasseypur. Meanwhile, his younger son, Faizal Khan, becomes influenced by Bollywood movies and takes on a different persona.

The feud between the Khan and Singh families intensifies, leading to more bloodshed and violence. The film explores the impact of this ongoing conflict on the lives of the characters.

In the end, Faizal Khan, now a mature and calculated individual, takes steps to avenge his family's suffering. The film reaches its climax with a violent showdown between the two families, leading to a resolution of sorts.

"Gangs of Wasseypur" is a sprawling and intense epic that delves deep into the world of organized crime, family, and revenge. It features a large ensemble cast and is known for its gritty and realistic portrayal of life in the coal-mining regions of India.
